About Me

I'm Michelle and I'm in the second year of my PhD in Psychology at the University of Granada with co-supervision from the HIP lab at the University of Oxford. My research explores how spatial memory and categorization learning interact, digging into the neural mechanisms behind them.

Before this, I studied Biomedical Engineering and Chemical & Biological Engineering at Colorado State University, where I got hooked on using machine learning and data science to understand the brain—especially cognition, aging, and learning. That passion also led me to pick up a minor in Mathematics.

Along the way, I’ve worked in both academia and industry, from research at Colorado State to a data science/engineering role at a SaaS/AI startup in Boulder. I’ve built expertise in neuroimaging analysis, computational modeling, product development, and even a bit of programming for online behavioral experiments.
